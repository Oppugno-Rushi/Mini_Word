Title: The Clockmaker of Evershade

In fog-wrapped Evershade, Old Bram kept time by whisper and pendulum—until one autumn night the clocktower stalled, the river held its breath, and he vanished, leaving a ring of tools around a brass key.

Found days later in the forest, he murmured, “Time has learned to watch us back,” and thereafter clocks wound themselves and aging went askew. Some say he bargained with the unseen, others that he wound eternity; when the moon turns red, the tower tolls thirteen—breath, not metal.
